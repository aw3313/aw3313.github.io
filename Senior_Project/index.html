    • Week 3: Responses

  • "Do The Work" by Steven Pressfield

    • The first thing that comes to mind when reading this chapter is the that don't resist your own creativity, don't be a hinderance to your own ideas. Pretty much what this chapter is saying to me is to "Just Do It" essentially. The first draft is the most authentic and real version of what you are creating. Don't worry if it's not perfect, doesn't make sense at first, just go with it. This is close to my heart, because I tend to be so judegemental with myself in terms of my creations. And its a bit easier to create a project when you have very specific guidelines. The difference between this class is that there are no specific guidelines, eveything is freeform. I create my own guidlines and that is scary for a college student. Filling in the gaps once the first draft is created makes more sense and brings me somewhat of a relief. I need to keep in mind not to be too hard on myself and to just build on from my first draft. From this reading I do believe it is better to start with action and then reflect on what you actually created, then you from the evalution you can created on top of what has already been built, or take away. Keep working and dont give up speaks volumes to me, I do whatever i takes to finish all my projects, I don't like to have a partly finish creation.

    • Week 7: Responses

  • "Do The Work" by Steven Pressfield

    • In all honesty, this reading to me was a bit too much for my liking. I can understand how resistance and one’s “Peripheral Opponents” are dangerous to a creative mindset. These two obstacles can definitely be warranted as dark loom shadow over the creative mind. However, the idea of seeing them as “dragons” and saying it’s a life-or-death experience is a bit much. I personally don’t take life to seriously and I don’t think anyone should be so serious on themselves, it isn’t the healthiest thing to do from my own experiences. The metaphors used in this specific reading about slaying a dragon to me is a bit much. What was interesting to me was the use of the word love, loving the project you are creating. Having that passion and drive to for a specific creation and/or project is familiar to me. Now one will not always have that passion for the project, but I guess you can have an overall passion for that creation.
